Intro to Consumer Technology Association

The Consumer Technology Association is an organization or business association that participates in holding events so that well-known brands like Amazon, Twitter, Samsung, Meta, LG, and many other tech businesses and startups may showcase their newest and most innovative consumer products. In order for those products to become more well-known and possibly lead to more consumer consumption. CTA’s headquarter is at 1919 S. Eads St., Arlington, Virginia, U.S. 22202, and it was formerly known as Consumer Electronics Association from 1999 to 2015. The CEO of CTS is Gary Shapiro.

Shows it Conducts

The Consumer Technology Association organizes events like the Consumer Electronics Show (CES) and CES Asia, where numerous firms from the consumer technology industry participate in an effort to announce their latest product launches and a preview of products. And it is held every January in Las Vegas. Additionally, this show attracts a growing number of attendees each year.

Brief History of CES

CES was first held in 1967 in New York City with 250 exhibitors and 17,500 attendees in New York City. Back then it was a spinoff of the Chicago Music Show and that was the time when the Music show served as a main event for showcasing consumer electronics and technologies. And the show was a huge success and year by year the number of tech companies exhibiting their products increased. Due to its popularity, the event was conducted twice a year from 1978 to 1994. Once in January in Las Vegas called Winter Consumer Electronics Show (WCES) and the second time it was held in June in Chicago called Summer Consumer Electronics Show. The Summer Consumer Electronics Show popularity was decreasing so the organizers changed it back to the annual format at the Las Vegas location. So, CES had been out there conducting events annually for the past 56 years, it is a record-breaking number to see a show running for such a long term.

Products Showcased at CES

A variety of products are showcased at CES, and those can be OLED, 4K, 8K, QLED, and flexible TVs from Samsung and LG and there are other products like drones, robots, the latest mobile GPUs, and laptops from Asus, Dell, Lenovo that use these GPUs. CES also showcases a variety of automobile concepts like cars running on hydrogen and the latest Electric vehicles that can change color from big brands like Audi, BMW, and polestar. CES event is also home for smart home devices and wearables. Click here to see the list of product categories presented at the CES event.

Mostly all these latest tech products may not come into the consumer market and get sold but we can briefly understand how this latest tech is going to change the future.

First Seen at CES

Understanding our surroundings is always crucial, and CES is one such occasion. You’ll be surprised to learn that numerous innovations, like the original VCR, CD player, DVD, HDTV, QLED, and Microsoft’s Xbox, were previously unveiled during CES gatherings.

  • 1969: The CES ‘69 demonstrated Panasonic’s 1.5-inch screen television and FM stereo headphones
  • 1970: Sony U-Matic VCR (Video Cassette Recorder) very first of its kind got introduced
  • 1971: An audio equipment that includes 8-track cassettes, and blank, recordable audio tape
  • 1974: world’s first Laserdisc player. And this was the year CES become a partner of NARM – National Association of Recording Merchandisers
  • 1975: Atari Pong game console
  • 1976: Cheap digital watches
  • 1977: first VHS (Video Home System), portable VCR
  • 1978: early home computers and dominated by music equipment
  • 1979: Atari’s 8-bit personal computers. The Atari 400 and Atari 800
  • 1981: CD (Compact Disk) and Camcorder
  • 1982: landmark Commodore 64 personal computer and Western Technologies Vectrex game console
  • 1984: Amiga home computer
  • 1985: Nintendo Entertainment System a home gaming console
  • 1988: the debut of the Tetris game
  • 1990: DAT (Digital Audio Technology) is present
  • 1992: was the last year Apple appeared at CES. John Sculley introduced Newton.
  • 1993: Sony introduced MiniDisc, a small storage disc player which is capable of holding 74 minutes of audio. Sony was manufacturing this until 2011. RDS (Radio Data System) was presented.
  • 1994: first Digital Satellite System is shown
  • 1995: Microsoft BOB
  • 1996: first DVD (Digital Versatile Disc) and Apple Pippin (Home video gaming console) was shown
  • 1998: HDTV (High-Definition Television) introduced for the first time
  • 1999: DVR (Digital Video Recorder) was introduced
  • 2000: first Digital Satellite Radio was introduced
  • 2001: Microsoft Xbox game console, this was the first gaming console made by Microsoft and this was the first time Microsoft entered the gaming console market.
  • 2002: The home media server is presented
  • 2003: first Blu-Ray was presented
  • 2004: HDA (High-Definition Audio) is presented
  • 2005: Windows Media Server was first introduced
  • 2008: Panasonic 150” Plasma TV and OLED TV

  • 2010: Intel Core i7 computer processor debuted, first tablet PC, netbook, and Android devices are introduced
  • 2011: Many other 3D TVs were introduced by Samsung, LG, and Toshiba
  • 2013: Qualcomm introduced snapdragon 600 & 800 processors that can give a capability to mobile phones to record 4K videos. Samsung introduced its Flexible OLED TV. 3D printers and Driverless car technology were also presented.
  • 2014: Samsung introduced curved TVs, and LG introduced Smart TVs with WebOS in them.
  • 2015: 4k compatible TVs were introduced
  • 2016: Huawei Watch for females with jewels on it, HTC Vive Pre, Faraday Future, 4k TVs from LG, Samsung, 4k Blu-Ray, Parrot Disco drone, Cota by Ossia wireless charging works like a Wi-Fi connection, BMW AirTouch, Inboard M1 Electric Longboard with enhanced features, Panasonic Transparent Display, LG Flexible screen which can be rolled and unrolled, Sensorwake Smell Alarm, and Ehang Autonomous Drone, Fitbit Blaze, LG Short Throw LED Home Theater Projecto, and DJI Phantom 3 4k were first introduced

  • 2018: Vivo’s Unveiled Integrated Fingerprint Technology, My Passport Wireless SSD with power and storage, Hyundai NEXO hydrogen fuel cell powered car, D-Link Wire-Free Full HD Camera, Polaroid user-friendly 3D printers, KOHLER connect, Cota by Ossia introduced wireless rechargeable battery, Unistellar eVscope, the Genovation GXE Sports the first mechanical Manual Transmission, and Netflix appeared at CES to market its new series Altered Carbon and many more.
  • 2019: Samsung introduced Foldable Smartphone, The Nreal Light a mixed reality glasses, OLED R by LG, Canon Rayo S1 Mini Projector, The Kohler Numi 2.0, The Flexible LG twinwash, The Capstone Smart Mirror, Somnox Sleep Robot, and many more.

Awards

The greatest innovations receive one of two categories of awards. One is the Honorees award, given to those who score higher than the cutoff for a particular category, and the other is the Best of Innovation award, given to the items with the highest ratings in each category.
